# Persona 1: Jimmy the programming student

## Background
Jimmy is a final year university student studying computer science and a self-taught web developer. He is highly tech-savvy and spend over 15 hours a week watching online video lectures, coding tutorials, and lectures on platforms like YouTube and university portals. While he understands core programming concepts, he relies heavily on video content to learn new frameworks and libraries. Alex is not good at time management, plus he has to balance a part-time job with a heavy study load, meaning efficiency in studying is his top priority.

## Goals
Why does this person use the application? What are they trying to achieve?
* **Active Learning:** Jimmy wants to test the code they see in tutorials immediately.
* **Information Retention:** He wants to quickly save important notes, codes or examples shown on recorded university lectures.
* **Streamlined Workflow:** He wants to seamlessly bridge the gap between passive video consumption and active coding without breaking his concentration. 

## Needs
What does this person need from the application? What are their pain points?
* **Pain Point (Manual Transcription):** Jimmy gets frustrated when videos has a complex block of code on screen for only a few seconds. He hates having to pause, manually type out the code, and inevitably hunt down syntax errors caused by typos.
* **Pain Point (Disruption):** Switching back and forth between a standard video player and a code editor breaks his concentration. 
* **Interaction Needs:** Jimmy needs a low-friction, highly responsive UI. The process of extracting text must be as fast as a keyboard shortcut or a single button click. The UI must clearly indicate when text has been successfully copied so he can confidently switch to his editor.


# Persona 2: Kim the data analyst 

## Background
Kim is a junior data analyst who has an impaired vision and relies heavily on a screen reader to navigate the web and write code. She is highly capable but she frequently encounter digital roadblocks. When taking online upskilling courses, she often encounter videos where the instructor presents data on screen without reading it aloud.

## Goals
**Independent Learning:** Kim wants to consume educational videos content without needing help to describe what is on the screen.

**Information Accessibility:** She wants to seamlessly convert inaccessible text in videos into structured text that screen readers can interpret.

## Needs
**Pain Point (Invisible Data):** If an instructor puts a block of code on the screen without speaking it, Morgan is completely locked out of that information.

**Pain Point (Mouse Dependency):** UIs that require precise mouse clicks to extract text are unusable for Morgan.

**Interaction Needs:** Morgan needs the application to be 100% keyboard navigable. She needs standard ARIA labels on all buttons so her screen reader announces "Extract Text from Video" when she tabs onto the button.
